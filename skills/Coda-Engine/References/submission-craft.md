## Rule zero
Do not submit until Coda's audit block reads SHIP with zero accepted failures.
Juries find the gap you shipped anyway.

## Where to submit, in order

### 1. Awwwards (first — fastest feedback loop)
- Rolling submissions; judged within ~1–2 weeks of going live in the queue
- Scored 1–10 by jury on Design / Usability / Creativity / Content; ~6.5+ =
  Honorable Mention, higher band = Site of the Day nomination
- Choose submission timing: avoid queue-heavy periods (post-Christmas, September);
  mid-week appearance days historically get steadier jury attention
- The public can also vote — the build-in-public audience is an asset here
- Categories/tags: pick precisely (e.g., "Startups", "Design agencies" is wrong —
  this is a product). Wrong tags = wrong jurors

### 2. CSS Design Awards / FWA (parallel or shortly after)
- Similar rolling model; a win here compounds the Awwwards result
- FWA skews technical/experiential — the Rubato behavior is the FWA hook

### 3. Webby Awards (the campaign, not the reflex)
- Annual cycle: entries typically open autumn, deadlines winter, winners spring
- Entry fees are substantial (hundreds of USD per category) — treat as a
  deliberate marketing spend, decide against runway
- Judged by IADAS on: Content, Structure & Navigation, Visual Design,
  Functionality, Interactivity, Overall Experience — the six areas map 1:1 onto
  Coda's surface tiers; run a fresh full Coda pass against these six headings
- "Webby People's Voice" is a public vote — the YouTube audience matters again

## The submission package (build all of it before submitting anywhere)

### Shots
- 1 hero shot: the Correction mid-strike (the exact frame where generic dies
  and real headline is entering) — this is the thumbnail jurors click or don't
- 3–5 detail shots: 404 page, proof-mark form error, margin annotation,
  focus-ring, ::selection — the quiet details ARE the differentiator shots;
  every submitter shows their hero, almost nobody shows their 404
- 1 short video/GIF (≤ 15s): Rubato axes responding to scroll velocity —
  motion that a static shot can't carry
- All shots on the real `#080f0a`, no mockup frames, no device chrome

### Copy
- Description written by the copywriting engine, aimed at jurors: lead with the
  thesis (positioning precedes everything; every pixel traces to brand.md),
  not the feature list. Aphoristic-confrontational register, one self-implicating
  line. ≤ 100 words — jurors skim
- One sentence naming the tech restraint as a choice: "No WebGL. 150KB. The
  slowness is the product."

### Credits
- Solo founder, open source, built in public — say so. Juries weight solo/small
  team work; it reframes the score

## Timing protocol
1. Ship the page for validation first (waitlist is the real metric)
2. Run 2 weeks of real-traffic hardening — fix what analytics and users surface
3. Fresh full Coda pass (Steps 1–6) on the hardened build
4. Submit Awwwards → capture the process on YouTube ("submitting to Awwwards
   in public") regardless of result — the content value is outcome-independent
5. Webby only if the annual window aligns and runway allows

## What a loss means
Awwwards publishes jury scores per axis. A sub-7 axis is a free expert audit:
feed it back as new entries in judged-surfaces.md, fix, resubmit next cycle.
The engine is designed to convert rejection into reference-file updates.
