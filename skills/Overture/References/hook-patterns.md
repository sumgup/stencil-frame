# hook-patterns.md
> Overture · reference file 02
> Loaded at Step 4. One hook per asset. Every hook must land in ≤ 3 seconds,
> survive sound-off, and read as a still frame. Emotion before explanation;
> clarity before cleverness.

---

## H-01 · Pattern Interrupt
- **What:** the first frame breaks the viewer's visual expectation of the
  medium — paper texture and a pencil line where they expected a SaaS gradient.
- **Why it works:** stops autopilot scrolling; our territory (Process-as-
  Provenance) is itself the interrupt against machine-smooth sameness.
- **Fits:** teaser, hero-loop.
- **Risk:** interrupt without follow-through reads as bait. Beat 2 must pay
  the interrupt off.

## H-02 · In Medias Res
- **What:** open mid-action — the strike-through already half-drawn, the
  stencil half-cut. The viewer arrives during the tension.
- **Why it works:** curiosity about completion; no setup tax.
- **Fits:** episode-open, teaser. Pairs with spine S-05.

## H-03 · The Named Enemy
- **What:** open on the thing the viewer secretly suspects — the template, the
  archetype quiz, the generic tagline — presented deadpan, no commentary.
- **Why it works:** recognition ("that's me") is the fastest emotional entry.
- **Fits:** hero-loop, episode-open.
- **Risk:** highest smugness risk of all hooks. Deadpan or dead. If D7 is not
  in the page's device budget, the enemy must be shown as copy, not imagery.

## H-04 · Curiosity Gap
- **What:** show an incomplete artifact whose completion the viewer wants —
  a question with no answer yet, a construction with a missing line.
- **Why it works:** open loops hold attention; resolution is the reward.
- **Fits:** demo, section-motion.
- **Risk:** never withhold past the placement's duration ceiling — a gap the
  asset doesn't close is a broken promise.

## H-05 · The Bold Claim
- **What:** the first frame is a single declarative line (copy-engine writes
  it), monumental type, nothing else.
- **Why it works:** clarity is the hook; Register D is built for it.
- **Fits:** hero-loop, teaser (large type survives vertical crops and mute).

## H-06 · Transformation Tease
- **What:** one frame of after-state shown first, then the story earns it.
- **Why it works:** stakes established instantly ("this is where you end up").
- **Fits:** episode-open. Pairs with spine S-04.
- **Guard:** the after-state is founder clarity, never a product screenshot.

---

## Hard rules (all hooks)
1. No slow fades, no logo stings, no "hi, my name is" openings.
2. The hook frame doubles as the fallback poster wherever possible — the
   still that must hook is the still the reduced-motion user gets.
3. If the hook needs audio or a caption to be understood, it failed Step 4.
4. Never stack patterns. One hook, then the spine takes over.
