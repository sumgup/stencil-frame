# Register: Marginalia (the second voice)
Status: ACTIVE — added 2026-07-24, following S+ confirmation.
Lineage: extension of the self-implicating register into a physical channel (Sumit's real handwriting).
Validation rider: first five concierge sessions double as passive field test; reopens only on evidence.

## What it is
A two-voice structure. The TYPESET voice carries the diagnosis (existing registers:
aphoristic-confrontational home base, self-implicating secondary). The HANDWRITTEN voice is a
second speaker in the margins — the human behind the machine, commenting on its own severity.
Severity and warmth stop competing for the same layer: the typeset voice never softens,
the hand never diagnoses.

## Hard rules
1. ALWAYS Sumit's real hand — one-off originals (tablet/SVG). Never a handwriting font on
   hero-level surfaces. A font regularizes; regularity is the machine-tell this voice exists to defeat.
   (Volume surfaces like D8-adjacent notes may use a private font built from Sumit's writing.)
2. Skippable by definition: an aside may never carry information required to understand the page.
   Remove every aside and the page must still work completely.
3. Ration: max 3 per screen. Scarcity is what keeps the voice a voice and not a texture.
4. Length: ≤ 6 words preferred, 10 absolute max. Lowercase. Periods allowed; exclamation marks never.
5. Stance: self-implicating or confessional — the hand laughs at US, never at the reader.
   It may puncture the typeset voice's authority; it may not undercut the argument.
6. Never explains the joke, never labels itself ("fun fact:", "real talk:" — banned).
7. Placement: physically attached to what it comments on (margins, arrows, under CTAs) —
   marginalia is positional by definition.
8. Accessibility: asides are real content — provide them as accessible text; arrows are decorative
   (aria-hidden). Below 768px, drop asides before shrinking them.

## Anti-slop pass (this register has the highest slop gravity in the system)
Banned vocabulary: unlock, journey, magic, magical, supercharge, effortless, seamless, elevate,
game-changer, vibe (except when quoting the false belief), haha, lol, tbh. No emojis in-hand.
Warmth mechanisms allowed: confession ("we tried this. twice."), understatement ("the slow way.
sorry."), promise-as-aside ("no mood boards. promise."), quiet admission of cost or doubt.
Warmth mechanisms banned: enthusiasm, encouragement, hype, compliments to the reader.

## Voice test (run on every aside)
- Could a competitor's brand say this line? → too generic, cut.
- Does it survive being read in a flat, tired voice? → if it needs pep to work, cut.
- Is it something Sumit would actually scribble on a printout? → if not, cut.

## Examples
GOOD: "we tried this. twice." / "the slow way. sorry." / "no mood boards. promise." /
"this took us six months to admit."
BAD: "you've got this!" (cheerleading, reader-directed) / "fun fact: positioning matters"
(labels itself, diagnoses) / "unlock your brand's magic ✨" (three banned words and an emoji).

## Relationship to the engine
No pipeline change. The six-step sequence is unchanged; marginalia is generated (or hand-written)
AFTER the typeset copy is final, as a commentary pass on it — never simultaneously, never as a
substitute for fixing weak typeset copy. If a section needs an aside to feel human, the typeset
copy has failed its own register first.
