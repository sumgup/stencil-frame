Status: draft
Last updated: 2026-07-16
Confidence: low (hypothesis only — no interviews, no VoC)
Priority: variant
Source count: 0

## One-line summary
An artist building something that expresses a worldview — like Basquiat, Frida Kahlo, or a street artist turned brand — who cares more about authenticity and voice than commercial polish.

## Primary Job-to-be-Done
When I'm building a creative practice/brand that needs to carry my values and voice into the world, I want to articulate what I stand for in a way that's honest and not corporate, so I can make art/products/content that people recognize as MINE.
- Functional job: Translate a worldview into brand decisions (merch, shows, collaborations, online presence)
- Emotional job: Feel like the brand IS the art, not a compromise of it
- Social job: Be taken seriously as an artist AND a brand — not selling out

## Circumstances & Trigger
- Push: Being asked to "brand yourself" in a way that feels inauthentic.
- Pull: Artists who successfully bridge art and commerce (Virgil Abloh, Kehinde Wiley, Yayoi Kusama).
- Habits: Resisting any systematization as "too commercial."
- Anxieties: "If I define my brand, I'll lose my artistic freedom / authenticity."

## Desired Transformation
Before: "I know what I believe but I can't package it without it feeling fake."
→ After: "The brand and the art are the same thing. I didn't compromise — I clarified."

## Beliefs
- "Branding is for corporations, not artists" → Reframe: Branding is knowing your voice. You already do that in your art. Now do it everywhere else.
- "Systematizing kills creativity" → Reframe: Constraints liberate. A positioning file is a creative constraint, like a canvas size.

## Objections
- "This sounds too business-y" → The tool doesn't ask about revenue or market share. It asks why you exist and what you stand for. Sound familiar?
- "I don't need a brand, I need to make art" → When someone else prints your art on a t-shirt and gets the vibe wrong, THAT'S a branding problem.

## Voice of Customer — Language
- Words THEY use: "my voice", "my vision", "authentic", "selling out", "staying true", "my practice", "my work"
- Words to AVOID: "brand kit", "deliverables", "KPIs", "conversion", "target audience"

## Copy Directives
- Tone: Most poetic of the three. Use art/music metaphors. Never corporate language.
- Proof: Show how brand.md can hold an artistic vision — not just commercial positioning.
