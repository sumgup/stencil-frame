# Persona: The Creative Entrepreneur
Status: draft
Last updated: 2026-07-16
Confidence: medium (derived from co-thinking session, not interviews yet)
Priority: lead
Source count: 0 interviews, 0 reviews mined, 1 co-thinking session

## One-line summary
A first-time founder building something they believe in, who feels their brand should mean something but can't yet articulate what — stuck in a loop of generating options without criteria to choose.

## Primary Job-to-be-Done
When I'm starting my first brand and every AI tool gives me options that all look the same, I want to figure out what my brand actually stands for, so I can make design and copy decisions with confidence instead of guessing.
- Functional job: Produce a coherent, usable brand identity (voice, visuals, copy)
- Emotional job: Stop feeling like a fraud who can't explain their own brand
- Social job: Be able to tell a designer, investor, or friend what I stand for — clearly

## Circumstances & Trigger
- Push: Canva/AI tools gave them outputs but no clarity. The loop of generate → feel nothing → generate again is exhausting.
- Pull: The idea that there's a "real" way to do branding — the way agencies do it — but accessible.
- Habits: Defaulting to surface decisions (picking fonts, palettes) because that feels like progress.
- Anxieties: "Maybe I'm just not cut out for this." "Maybe real founders just know their brand intuitively."

## Desired Transformation
Before: Staring at two color palettes at midnight, unable to explain why either is right. Lonely — nobody understands why this is so hard.
→ After: One file that holds their positioning. Every decision traceable. Able to hand a designer a file and say "this is what I stand for."

## Awareness Stage & Sophistication
- Default stage: Problem-Aware but wrongly diagnosed (thinks the problem is aesthetics; actual problem is positioning)
- By channel:
  - Instagram/TikTok: Unaware to Problem-Aware (sees brand content, feels envy, doesn't know why theirs doesn't work)
  - Google search: Problem-Aware ("how to build a brand identity", "brand kit template")
  - Landing page (organic): Problem-Aware to Solution-Aware
  - Landing page (paid): Problem-Aware (cold traffic)
- Market sophistication: 4/5 (high — has seen "build your brand in 5 minutes" many times)
- Copy implication: Direct claims won't land. Must name what they're feeling, then reframe the problem. Mechanism/story leads, not benefit leads.

## Beliefs (what they currently think — even if wrong)
- "Brand = aesthetics (logo, palette, fonts, tagline)" → Reframe: Brand = positioning first, aesthetics derive from it
- "I need to figure out my brand aesthetic/vibe" → Reframe: You need to figure out why you exist, then the aesthetic follows
- "AI tools can solve this for me" → Reframe: AI gave you more options, not more clarity
- "Good founders just know their brand intuitively" → Reframe: Nobody does — they just had the process (or an agency that did)

## Objections
- "This sounds like a lot of work" → Proof: It IS more work. But it's the work that makes every future decision easier.
- "Why can't AI just do this for me?" → Proof: AI generated your last 10 options. How did that work out?
- "I'm too early for branding" → Proof: You're not too early for knowing why you exist. That's the earliest possible step.

## Success Metrics (how they judge "did this work?")
- "I can explain my brand in one sentence" → Show: the brand.md one-liner
- "I can make a design decision and explain why" → Show: audit trail
- "A designer understood my brand from one file" → Show: brand.md handoff story
- "I stopped second-guessing every choice" → Show: the decision criteria in brand.md

## Category Entry Points (when they think of this category)
- When: Starting a new project and realizing they need a "brand"
- When: A designer asks "what do you stand for?" and they can't answer
- When: Comparing their brand to a competitor's and feeling inadequate
- Where: Late at night, alone, staring at options
- Why: The gap between what they feel and what they can articulate

## Voice of Customer — Language
- Words THEY use: "vibe", "aesthetic", "brand identity", "look and feel", "mood board", "brand kit", "brand guidelines"
- Words to AVOID (they wouldn't say these): "positioning", "brand architecture", "value proposition", "category entry points", "market sophistication"
- Sticky phrases: "I just want it to feel right" / "I don't know how to explain what I want" / "I keep changing my mind"

## Copy Directives (derived from above)
- Marketing tone: SYM-dominant, GAV-accented (diagnostic with sharp teeth — the doctor who names who caused the illness)
- Microcopy/UX tone:
  - Onboarding: Warm, direct, no jargon — "We're going to ask you some hard questions. That's the point."
  - Errors: Blame-free, human — "That answer's too vague. Try again — what do you actually mean?"
  - Empty states: Encouraging but honest — "Nothing here yet. The good kind of empty."
  - CTAs: Outcome-framed — "Start with positioning" not "Sign up"
- Proof type priority: demo (show the session) > audit trail > story > testimonial > data
- Copy length tendency: Medium-long for cold/problem-aware. Short for product-aware.
