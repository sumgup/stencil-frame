# Stencil + Frame — Investigate Step

## Conceptual Design (Step 1)

_Completed: May 2026_

---

## Core Principle

Brand is identity and values, not just market mechanics. The "why we're here" question is the hero — not optional. Every session must hold space for emotional and values-driven brand creation alongside gap analysis.

Gap without identity is capitalism. Identity without gap is noise. Both must coexist.

_Inspired by Jacqueline Novogratz — Manifesto for a Moral Revolution: brands that carry moral imagination see the world as it is while imagining what it could be. They ask not just who their customer is, but what those people deserve that they are not getting today._

---

## Session Name

**"Find Your Gap"**

Plain language. No jargon. Tells the user exactly what they are doing.

---

## Design Principles

- **Co-thinking over form-filling.** The session is a conversation, not a questionnaire.
- **No jargon.** Plain questions only. No "brand equity", "value proposition", "positioning matrix."
- **Show don't instruct.** Watermark text in input fields shows a real example (Stencil + Frame's own answer) before the user types anything.
- **Honest about limits.** Stencil never oversells. A gap is a possibility, not a guarantee.
- **One follow-up maximum per act.** Don't let any act become a therapy session.
- **Meet the user where they are.** A user building a bakery answers simply. A user building a movement goes deep. The agent never demands a manifesto — it creates space for one.

---

## Two Modes

At the very start the agent asks one question:

_"Is this brand new, or does it already exist in some form?"_

- **New brand** — starts fresh, no history to audit. Skips Past/Present/Future exercise.
- **Existing brand** — has perception baggage. Includes Past/Present/Future exercise in Act 1.

Same destination. Different starting point.

---

## Honest Framing — Before Act 0 Begins

One quiet line shown before the session starts:

_"Finding your gap is the beginning, not the answer. This session will help you see clearly — what you do with that is up to you."_

## Act 0 — Who Are You?

**The internal investigation. Happens before everything else.**

### What it does

This is the soul of the session. Before looking outward at the market, the user looks inward. Who are they, what do they believe, what do they want to leave behind. The gap means nothing without this. Anyone can find white space. Only you can fill it in your particular way.

### How it works

Three questions. Asked slowly. The agent does not rush.

**Question 1 — What do you do?**

Plain and functional. One sentence. The agent listens for clarity. If the answer is vague or generic it gently asks: _"Can you say that without using any industry words?"_

**Question 2 — What do you believe?**

This is values. The agent asks:

_"What makes you angry about how things are done in your space today? What exists that shouldn't — or what should exist that doesn't?"_

If the answer is shallow the agent pushes once: _"What would you change if you had no constraints?"_

**Question 3 — Who are you really here for, and what do they deserve that they're not getting today?**

This is purpose and moral imagination combined. Not "target audience." Real people. What is the world like for them right now, and what could it be?

The agent sits with this answer. It reflects back. It does not move forward until this feels honest.

### The Johnson shortcut

If a user can answer all three questions clearly, honestly, and without contradiction — they may not need the full Investigate session. The agent acknowledges this:

_"You seem to have a clear sense of who you are and why you're here. That's rare. Let's check if the market agrees."_

Then moves directly to Act 2.

### What Act 0 produces

- A values statement — what the brand believes in plain language
- A purpose statement — who it is really for and what they deserve
- A one-line identity — what it does, stripped of jargon

These three feed into Act 3 and become the identity layer of `brand.md`.

## Act 1 — Warm Up

**Framework: Facts / Obstacles / Opportunities**

### What it does

Gets the user out of their head and into the open. Organises what they already carry. The agent's job here is not to ask — it is to organise what the user already knows.

### How it works

Agent opens with one question:

_"Tell me about your brand. What is it, and what problem does it solve?"_

User responds freely. Agent listens and organises into three buckets — facts, obstacles, opportunities — then reflects back:

_"Here's what I'm hearing. These seem like the facts... this feels like the main obstacle... and here's where I think the opportunity might be hiding. Does that feel right?"_

User corrects or confirms. Agent updates.

### Input field watermark

Pre-filled example text showing Stencil + Frame's own answer. Sets the bar without lecturing.

_Example watermark: "DIY branding for people starting fresh. Problem: Coherent voice and visuals from Day 1 — avoid the beginner trap, kill impostor syndrome."_

### Act 1 produces

- A one-line description of the brand
- The main obstacle in plain language
- A rough sense of where the opportunity might be

## Act 2 — The Gap Map

**Two sub-steps: Research → Map**

### Act 2a — Research

**How it works:** Stencil takes Act 1 outputs and slots them into a pre-written prompt template (string substitution — no AI needed). Generates a structured research prompt in three parts:

**Part A — Settings instruction** Plain and specific. Example: _"Open Perplexity or ChatGPT. Turn on web search or deep research mode. Create a new conversation. Paste the prompt below."_

**Part B — The research prompt** Instructs the user to find 5–8 competitors. For each: what they do, who they serve, how they position themselves, what users complain about. Includes adjacent tools — not just obvious names.

**Part C — What to bring back** _"Copy the full response and paste it back into Stencil."_

**After results are pasted:** Stencil structures the output into competitor cards. Each card has:

- Brand name in strong typography
- One-line descriptor (agent-generated)
- Dominant colour from their website
- Position on the two-axis map

No logos in v1. User can add, remove, or edit any card before moving forward.

**Act 2a ends when** the competitor list is confirmed by the user.

---

### Act 2b — Map

**How it works:** Agent looks at the confirmed competitor list and proposes 2–3 axis pair variations based on what it sees in the research. For example:

- Visual execution → Strategic thinking / Assumes brand exists → Builds from scratch
- DIY → Agency-led / Cheap → Premium
- Fast → Considered / Generic → Values-driven

User picks the axis pair that feels most true to their market. They can also define their own axes entirely if none feel right.

Map is drawn. Everyone plotted. Gap named.

Agent asks: _"This space is empty — [description]. Is that where you want to be?"_

User confirms. Gap locked. One map per brand.

### Stencil + Frame live example

- X axis: Visual execution → Strategic thinking
- Y axis: Assumes brand exists → Builds from scratch
- Gap: Top right quadrant. Empty. Nobody starts at the beginning.
- Gap sentence: _"The only tool that helps you think before you build."_

### Act 2 produces

- Confirmed competitor list with cards
- Two axes that define the market
- The gap — one plain sentence

## Act 3 — Confirm

### What it does

Unifies identity and gap into one statement. The session started with who the user is (Act 0) and found where they fit (Act 2). Act 3 brings these together. The confirmation is not just "here is your gap" — it is "here is your gap, and here is why you specifically are the right person to fill it."

### How it works

Agent reflects everything back in one clean summary:

- **Who you are** — values, purpose, identity from Act 0
- **The landscape** — who exists and where they cluster
- **The gap** — one plain sentence
- **The connection** — why your identity and this gap belong together

Then asks one question:

_"Does this feel true? Is there anything here that doesn't sit right?"_

User corrects or confirms. Stencil writes this into `brand.md`.

### Honest closing note

After confirmation, before moving to Strategy:

_"A gap is a possibility, not a guarantee. What you've found today is worth investigating further — that's exactly what the next step is for."_

### What Act 3 is NOT

No confetti. No celebration screen. The confirmation should feel quiet and solid — like something real just got written down.

### Act 3 produces

The Research layer of `brand.md` with these fields locked:

**Identity layer**

- Values statement
- Purpose statement
- Brand one-liner

**Research layer**

- Competitor landscape summary
- Gap statement
- The two axes that revealed the gap
- Why this identity belongs in this gap

## AI Strategy — Option B (Prompt Export)

No AI embedded in v1. Stencil generates research prompts via string substitution from Act 1 outputs. User runs the prompt in their AI tool of choice and pastes results back.

- Zero infrastructure cost
- Keeps Stencil AI-agnostic as stated in the spec
- The prompt itself is a product — a well-designed research prompt is genuinely valuable
- Upgrade path to bring-your-own-key in v2

## Session Flow

```
Honest framing shown
        ↓
Mode selection (new brand / existing brand)
        ↓
Act 0 — Who are you? (values + purpose + identity)
        ↓
Act 1 — Warm up (facts / obstacles / opportunities)
        ↓
Act 2a — Research (prompt export → paste results → confirm competitor cards)
        ↓
Act 2b — Map (agent proposes axis pairs → user selects or defines own → plot → name the gap)
        ↓
Act 3 — Confirm (identity + gap unified → write to brand.md)
        ↓
Honest closing note → proceed to Strategy step
```
